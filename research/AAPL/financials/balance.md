## Observations

- **Negative working capital by design.** Apple's current ratio consistently below 1.0 is intentional — the company collects from customers (via carriers and retail) faster than it pays suppliers. This is a sign of supply chain dominance, not financial weakness.
- **Debt reduction in progress.** Total debt down from $101B (FY2023) to $86B (FY2025) as Apple lets bonds mature without full replacement. Net leverage is minimal at 0.22x EBITDA.
- **Equity recovery.** Shareholders' equity rebounded from $57B to $74B in FY2025, despite massive buybacks, driven by strong retained earnings. Apple had previously driven equity negative through buybacks.
- **Capital-light model.** PP&E is only $50B for a $416B revenue business — ~12% asset intensity. Apple outsources manufacturing to Foxconn, Pegatron, etc.
- **Minimal goodwill.** Apple does not carry material goodwill or intangible assets — almost all growth is organic, not acquisition-driven.
