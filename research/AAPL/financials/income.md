## Notes

- Apple's FY2024 had an unusually high tax rate (24.1%) due to a one-time tax charge related to European Commission rulings. Normalizing for this, underlying net income growth was positive.
- Share count declining ~2.5-3% annually from buybacks, amplifying EPS growth above net income growth.
- Gross margin expansion reflects Services mix shift (~75% gross margin vs. ~37% for Products) and Apple Silicon cost efficiencies.
- R&D spend increasing as a % of revenue — reflecting AI/ML investment (Apple Intelligence) and Vision Pro development.
