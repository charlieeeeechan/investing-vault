## Observations

- **FCF machine.** Apple generates ~$100B+ in annual FCF with minimal capital requirements. CapEx is only 2-3% of revenue — Apple outsources hardware manufacturing.
- **FCF conversion dipped in FY2025.** FCF/Net Income dropped to 88% from 116% — driven by working capital changes (higher receivables from Q1 ramp, lower payables timing). Not structural.
- **Buyback dominance.** Share repurchases consume ~80-90% of FCF. Diluted shares declined from 15.8B (FY2023) to 15.0B (FY2025), a ~5% reduction in 2 years.
- **Returning more than it earns.** Total shareholder returns exceeded FCF in FY2024 and FY2025. Apple is drawing down its cash hoard and letting debt mature — intentional balance sheet optimization.
- **CapEx rising.** FY2025 CapEx increased to $12.7B (+35% YoY), likely reflecting data center buildout for Apple Intelligence and Vision Pro manufacturing.
- **SBC growing.** Stock-based compensation up 19% over 3 years ($10.8B to $12.9B) — worth monitoring as a real cost partially offset by buybacks.
