# AAPL — Financial Insights

*Last updated: 2026-03-12*
*Source: SEC EDGAR 10-K filings (FY2021–FY2025)*

---

## Key Takeaways from FY2025 (ended Sep 2025)

1. **Revenue reaccelerated to 6.4% growth** after a flat FY2023 (-2.8%) and modest FY2024 (+2.0%). This was the strongest growth in three years, driven by iPhone 16 cycle and services expansion.

2. **Net income jumped 19.5% to $112B** — significantly outpacing revenue growth. This was driven by gross margin expansion (46.9% vs 46.2%) and operating leverage.

3. **EPS surged 22.7% to $7.46** — the combination of higher net income and continued buybacks (~2.6% share reduction) amplified per-share growth.

4. **Gross margin trend is remarkable** — expanding from 41.8% in FY2021 to 46.9% in FY2025. This is the services mix shift in action: services carry ~70%+ gross margins, and as services grow faster than products, the blended margin expands.

5. **CapEx spiked 34.6% to $12.7B** — a notable increase after years of ~$10-11B. This likely reflects investment in AI infrastructure (data centers for Apple Intelligence cloud processing). This is a trend to watch.

## Strengths

- **Cash generation machine:** $111B CFO, $99B FCF in FY2025. Apple generates more free cash flow than most companies generate in revenue.
- **Margin expansion trajectory:** Gross margin has expanded ~500bps over 5 years with room to continue as services mix grows.
- **Balance sheet deleveraging:** Net debt declined from $75B to $55B over 2 years while still returning ~$100B/year to shareholders.
- **Buyback consistency:** ~3% annual share reduction has been a powerful EPS compounder — turning low-single-digit revenue growth into high-single-digit EPS growth.

## Concerns

- **Revenue growth is modest for the valuation:** 5yr revenue CAGR of ~2.6% and 3yr CAGR of ~1.8% is slow for a company trading at 30x+ earnings. The stock prices in significant growth acceleration.
- **CFO declined in FY2025:** Operating cash flow dropped 5.7% despite 19.5% net income growth. Working capital or timing effects may be at play — worth investigating.
- **CapEx step-up may be structural:** If Apple Intelligence requires ongoing heavy infrastructure spending, FCF margins may compress from their historical 25-28% range.
- **China remains an uncertainty:** ~17% revenue exposure to a market facing both competitive (Huawei) and geopolitical headwinds.

## Questions to Watch

- Will Apple Intelligence drive a measurable upgrade cycle in FY2026? Early signals from iPhone 16 suggest modest demand pull, but the AI features are still rolling out.
- Can services growth sustain double-digit rates as the base gets larger?
- How does the EU DMA and US DOJ antitrust case progress? App Store take-rate reduction could directly hit the highest-margin revenue stream.
- Is the CapEx increase a one-year phenomenon or the start of a new, higher spending baseline?
- How will tariff escalation impact product margins? Apple has limited pricing power in some markets.
