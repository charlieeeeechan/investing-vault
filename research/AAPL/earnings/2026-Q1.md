# AAPL — Q1 FY2026 Earnings Analysis

**Quarter ended:** December 28, 2025
**Reported:** January 29, 2026
**Source:** Q1 FY2026 Earnings Release, 8-K filing (SEC EDGAR)

---

## Headline Numbers

| Metric | Q1 FY2026 | Q1 FY2025 | YoY Change |
| ------ | --------: | --------: | ---------: |
| Revenue | $143,844M | $124,300M | +15.7% |
| Gross Profit | $69,293M | $57,963M | +19.5% |
| Operating Income | $50,886M | $42,832M | +18.8% |
| Net Income | $42,065M | $36,330M | +15.9% |
| EPS (Diluted) | $2.84 | $2.40 | +18.3% |
| Gross Margin | 48.2% | 46.6% | +160 bps |
| Operating Margin | 35.4% | 34.5% | +90 bps |

**Verdict:** All-time record quarterly revenue. Beat on every major metric. EPS growth (+18.3%) outpaced revenue growth (+15.7%) due to margin expansion + buyback tailwind.

---

## Key Highlights

- **Record revenue across every major geography** — Americas, Europe, Greater China, Japan, Rest of Asia Pacific all posted records.
- **iPhone revenue records in all geographies** — strongest iPhone quarter since launch. AI upgrade cycle appears to be materializing.
- **Services all-time record** — approximately +14% YoY, continuing the acceleration trend.
- **Installed base exceeded 2.5B active devices** — new all-time high. Each device is a recurring revenue opportunity.
- **Gross margin expanded to 48.2%** — up from 46.9% for full-year FY2025. Services mix shift and Apple Silicon efficiencies driving expansion toward 50%.

## Guidance & Outlook

Management did not provide explicit revenue guidance (Apple typically does not give specific forward guidance). Key commentary from earnings call:

- Expect Services to continue growing at a strong double-digit rate
- Apple Intelligence expanding to more languages and countries in coming months
- CapEx expected to increase modestly as Apple invests in AI infrastructure
- Confident in long-term opportunity from 800M+ iPhones over 4 years old

## Thesis Impact

| Thesis Element | Impact | Assessment |
| -------------- | ------ | ---------- |
| AI upgrade cycle | Strongly positive | iPhone records across all geographies suggest AI features are driving upgrades |
| Services growth | Positive | ~14% YoY at scale confirms the flywheel. Path to $100B+ annual run rate |
| Margin expansion | Strongly positive | 48.2% gross margin — beat expectations. Structural trend intact |
| China risk | Neutral | Record revenue in Greater China this quarter, but one quarter doesn't resolve structural risk |
| Valuation | Challenging | Strong results likely push multiple higher. Harder to find margin of safety |

**Overall:** Q1 FY2026 was a validation quarter. The AI upgrade cycle thesis is playing out. Services continues to compound. Margins expanding. The question is whether the market has already priced this in at ~35x P/E.

---

## What to Watch

1. **Sustainability of iPhone momentum.** Q1 is seasonally the strongest (holiday quarter). Need Q2/Q3 to confirm this is a cycle, not a blip.
2. **Apple Intelligence adoption metrics.** Apple has not disclosed usage data. Engagement metrics would help quantify the AI impact.
3. **Services growth durability.** ~14% at $100B+ run rate is impressive. Watch for deceleration as base effects kick in and regulatory headwinds mount (EU DMA sideloading, Google search deal).
4. **CapEx trajectory.** Management flagged "modest increase" — need specifics. A $5B+ step-up would signal meaningful AI infrastructure buildout.
5. **Greater China follow-through.** Record Q1, but Huawei Mate 70 launched in November. Competitive dynamics in China remain fluid.
