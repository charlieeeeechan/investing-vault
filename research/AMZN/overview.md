## Business Overview

Amazon operates as a diversified technology and retail company across three reportable segments:

1. **North America** — Retail sales of consumer products (including from third-party sellers) and advertising in North America. Includes Amazon.com, Prime membership, and Whole Foods.

2. **International** — Same as North America but for international markets. Operates country-specific websites across Europe, Asia, Latin America, and the Middle East.

3. **Amazon Web Services (AWS)** — Cloud computing platform offering compute, storage, database, machine learning, and other services on a pay-as-you-go basis. The dominant public cloud provider globally.

### Revenue Mix (approximate, FY2025)
- North America: ~60% of revenue
- International: ~23% of revenue
- AWS: ~17% of revenue (but generates disproportionate operating income)

### Key Revenue Streams
- **E-commerce (1P):** Direct product sales
- **Third-party seller services:** Fulfillment, marketplace fees, commissions
- **AWS:** Cloud infrastructure and platform services
- **Advertising:** Sponsored products, display, video ads (one of the fastest-growing segments)
- **Subscription services:** Prime membership, Kindle Unlimited, Audible
- **Physical stores:** Whole Foods, Amazon Go, Amazon Fresh

## Investment Thesis

Amazon is a structurally advantaged platform business with dominant positions in e-commerce (US ~40% share), cloud computing (AWS ~31% share), and digital advertising (~7% share and growing). The bull case centers on AWS re-acceleration driven by AI/ML workloads, advertising margin expansion, and continued operating leverage from the retail business hitting maturity. The bear case focuses on massive capital intensity ($132B CapEx in FY2025, primarily for data centers), regulatory risk, and the question of whether current multiples fairly discount the required investment to sustain growth.

**Confidence: Medium** — The business quality is unquestionable, but valuation at ~30x trailing earnings requires sustained high growth and margin expansion to justify.

---

## Bull Case

1. **AWS + AI tailwind:** Generative AI is driving a massive wave of cloud infrastructure demand. AWS is investing aggressively in custom chips (Trainium, Inferentia) and Bedrock (managed AI service) to capture this. AI could extend AWS's growth runway for years.
2. **Advertising is a margin engine:** Amazon's advertising business (est. $60B+ run-rate) is high-margin and growing 20%+ annually. It monetizes existing traffic and data at very high incremental margins.
3. **Retail operating leverage:** After years of heavy investment in fulfillment regionalization and same-day delivery, Amazon's North America retail margins have expanded sharply (from breakeven in 2022 to strong profitability by 2024-2025). Further leverage is possible.
4. **International profitability inflection:** International segment has been a persistent drag but is reaching scale in key markets (India, Europe). Turning profitable would unlock significant incremental value.
5. **FCF inflection after CapEx cycle:** The current data center build cycle is historically intense. When it normalizes, FCF should expand dramatically given the high-margin revenue it generates.

## Bear Case

1. **Capital intensity is extreme:** $132B CapEx in FY2025, with guidance suggesting continued heavy spending. This reduces near-term FCF to near zero and creates execution risk if AI demand disappoints.
2. **AWS growth deceleration risk:** Despite AI demand, AWS growth has decelerated from 30%+ to mid-teens. Competitive pressure from Azure and GCP is real. Capacity constraints have limited growth.
3. **Regulatory and antitrust risk:** FTC lawsuit, EU Digital Markets Act, potential breakup pressure. Amazon faces regulatory scrutiny globally on marketplace practices, labor, and data.
4. **Valuation assumes a lot:** At ~30x trailing P/E and 300x P/FCF, the stock prices in substantial future improvement. If margins plateau or growth slows more than expected, significant downside exists.
5. **SBC dilution:** Stock-based compensation has been ~$20-24B annually. While declining as a percentage of revenue, it represents real economic cost that reduces per-share value.

## Key Metrics to Watch

- **AWS revenue growth rate** — The single most important KPI. Re-acceleration above 20% would be very bullish.
- **AWS operating margin** — Has fluctuated between 24-37%. Trend direction matters for thesis.
- **CapEx trajectory** — When does the AI build cycle peak? Normalization would unlock massive FCF.
- **Advertising revenue growth** — High-margin revenue. Sustained 20%+ growth is thesis-supporting.
- **North America operating margin** — Continuation of improvement toward 6-8% would be very positive.
- **International segment profitability** — Breakeven-to-profitable transition is a key catalyst.
- **Free cash flow generation** — FCF less CapEx. Currently near zero; the trajectory here defines the stock.
