# AMZN — Analytical Insights

*Based on SEC EDGAR 10-K filings (FY2021–FY2025)*
*Last updated: 2026-03-09*

---

## Key Takeaways

### 1. Margin Expansion Story Is Real
Amazon's operating margin has expanded from 2.4% (FY2022 trough) to 11.2% (FY2025) — a dramatic improvement driven by:
- Fulfillment network regionalization reducing delivery costs
- Advertising revenue growth (high-margin incremental revenue)
- AWS operating leverage
- Reduced SBC as a percentage of revenue (3.8% → 2.7%)

Gross margin has expanded by 830bps over 4 years (42.0% → 50.3%), suggesting structural improvement rather than a one-time effect.

### 2. CapEx Intensity Is the Central Tension
FY2025 CapEx of $131.8B (18.4% of revenue) is staggering. This is almost entirely driven by data center buildout for AI workloads. Key questions:
- **Is this the right bet?** If AI demand materializes as expected, these data centers generate high-margin recurring revenue for 15+ years.
- **When does it peak?** Management hasn't signaled a plateau. If FY2026 CapEx exceeds $150B, FCF will remain minimal.
- **What's the payback period?** AWS generates ~30% operating margins on revenue from these assets. A $130B investment generating 30% margins on eventual revenue of ~$150B+/year would justify the spend.

### 3. CFO Strength Masks the FCF Problem
CFO has tripled from $46B (FY2021) to $139B (FY2025). This is a sign of enormous underlying business strength. But FCF dropped to just $7.7B because CapEx consumed nearly all of it. Investors need to take a view on whether current CapEx is:
- **Growth CapEx** (will generate future returns) → use CFO or owner earnings as the valuation metric
- **Maintenance CapEx** (required to sustain the business) → FCF is the right metric

**Opinion:** Most of the current CapEx is growth CapEx (new data centers, AI infrastructure). Maintenance CapEx for the retail business is likely ~$30-40B. This implies "normalized" FCF of ~$100B+, which would put the stock at ~23x normalized FCF.

### 4. Balance Sheet Is Rock Solid
Net cash position of $18B despite the CapEx binge. Debt/equity of 0.17x is the lowest in years. Amazon could lever up significantly if needed, giving it enormous financial flexibility.

### 5. SBC Is Declining as a % of Revenue
SBC peaked at 4.2% of revenue (FY2023) and has fallen to 2.7% (FY2025). This is positive for per-share economics. However, at $19.5B annually, it remains a meaningful cost that reduces FCF available to shareholders.

## Structural Strengths

- **Diversified revenue streams:** E-commerce, cloud, advertising, and subscriptions provide resilience.
- **Operating leverage:** Fixed-cost infrastructure scales with revenue growth, expanding margins.
- **Cash generation:** $139B CFO in FY2025 is among the highest of any company globally.
- **Reinvestment runway:** AI/ML, healthcare (One Medical, Amazon Pharmacy), logistics (delivery drones, robots), and international expansion provide years of reinvestment opportunities.

## Concerns & Risks

- **Massive CapEx with uncertain returns:** If AI demand disappoints or commoditizes faster than expected, the ROI on $130B+ CapEx could be poor.
- **Regulatory overhang:** FTC antitrust suit, EU DMA compliance, and potential breakup rhetoric create ongoing uncertainty.
- **Retail margin ceiling:** North America operating margin has improved sharply, but how much further can it go? Competition from Walmart, Temu, and Shein pressures pricing.
- **International remains subscale:** International segment has historically been a drag. Profitability there is still fragile.
- **Tax rate normalization:** Effective tax rate was low in prior years. FY2025 jumped to 21.3%. If this normalizes higher, net income growth slows.

## Questions to Watch in Future Quarters

1. What is AWS's revenue growth trajectory? Is it re-accelerating?
2. When will management signal a CapEx plateau?
3. What is the operating margin trajectory by segment?
4. How is the advertising business performing vs. peers (Google, Meta)?
5. Any updates on the FTC antitrust case?
6. Is international segment sustaining profitability?
